# 10 Style Tests · Same Topic, 10 Voices

**Topic:** Prečo šetrením nikdy nezbohatneš (a čo robiť namiesto toho)

**Why this topic for the test:**
- Every SK/CZ reader has been told "šetri" by parents, school, banks, news. Daniel saying the opposite is an instant pattern interrupt.
- It immediately positions Daniel as someone who thinks differently from mainstream Slovak personal finance writers (Robert Vlach, finex, peniaze.sk).
- The natural answer to "if not saving, then what?" is: tvor hodnotu / online produkt / digital business — which is exactly what PDF Metóda teaches. CTA is organic.
- The contrarian framing creates the strongest "aha moment" potential for a flagship piece.

**Research foundation used:**
- Jeremy Haynes marketing playbook (hook structures, emotional triggers, CTA psychology)
- PDF Mastermind transcript (Daniel's actual voice, phrases, examples)
- Top performers internationally in this niche: Naval Ravikant, MJ DeMarco, Tim Ferriss, Hormozi, Justin Welsh, Mr Money Mustache, Paul Graham
- Pattern observed: best money blogs have ONE big contrarian idea per post, ONE personal anchor (story or number), ONE clear next action.

**The 10 styles:**

| # | Style | Best for | Read time |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | Naval — aphoristic, dense | Twitter audience, philosophy-curious readers | 3 min |
| 02 | Personal narrative | Emotional connection, building trust | 6 min |
| 03 | Numbered listicle | SEO, mainstream readers, scanners | 5 min |
| 04 | Contrarian polemic | Confident challenge, viral potential | 5 min |
| 05 | Socratic Q&A | Readers with specific objections | 5 min |
| 06 | Step-by-step practical | Action-takers, "show me what to do" readers | 6 min |
| 07 | Letter to younger self | Reflective, intimate, founder voice | 7 min |
| 08 | Data-driven analysis | Skeptics, analytical readers, math people | 6 min |
| 09 | Paul Graham essay | Thinkers, long-form readers | 8 min |
| 10 | Hormozi hook-loop | Short attention, creator economy reader | 4 min |

**The CTA — exactly what was specified:**

Every article ends with a short, honest CTA:
> "Všetko, čo som sa za roky naučil, som vložil do jedného kurzu — PDF Metóda. Predávam ho za €27. Pred kúpou si však pozri **zadarmo video**, kde celý systém vysvetľujem od A po Z. [Pozri si video →]"

Course price: **€27**. Free thing offered: **video that explains the system**. Honest framing — not pretending the course is free.

**How to evaluate:**

Read each article. Notice which voice you'd actually want to write 500 articles in over the next year. Pick on:
1. Which voice feels most like you?
2. Which one would you enjoy writing the most?
3. Which one creates the strongest aha moment?
4. Which one hooks you on paragraph 1?

Tell me the winner, and I'll lock that style as the editorial direction for the entire blog.
